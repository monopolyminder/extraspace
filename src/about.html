Fair Questions About This Site

Questions I'd ask. Answered plainly.

Who runs this?

A customer whose story is below. I simply do not trust a company with the demonstrated moral failings of Extra Space not to pursue frivolous litigation just for speaking out against them. Is anyone paying for this?

Just me. This site is self-funded. There are no ads, no affiliate links, no donation button, and nothing for sale. No law firm, competitor, or industry group had any hand in it. Just one of their customers who knows what a bait-and-switch scheme looks like.

What happens if I email my story?

Nothing gets published without explicit permission from the sender. I will do my best to confirm the details of your experience, and only publish details you are comfortable with and agree to share. You can remain anonymous. Your stories power the movement by rebuilding the public record and exposing the human cost of these exploitative practices.

Isn't "scam" too strong a word?

No court has ruled it one, and the word here is an opinion. One formed after reviewing a city lawsuit that called the pricing model a "classic bait-and-switch scheme," along with thousands of complaints describing the same sequence. Read the sources and decide for yourself.

My Story

My Extra Space Experience

The Start
My experience started out ordinary. A rainy day exposed a leak that left my belongings sitting in water. No apology. The office offered me another unit away from the leak.
My Shock
They then turned around and rented the leaking unit, still unrepaired, to the next person. In the weeks that followed, I watched that renter's belongings and the belongings in the units around it get ruined the same way. I saw a woman standing in that hallway crying over what she had lost. It was terrible to witness, and all of it traced back to a leak the company knew about and did not care enough to fix. When I told the front desk I would leave a review about what happened, I was told that if I left a review I would have 48 hours to clear out every unit on my account, at every location. Knowing I could not comply with such a request at the time, I stayed quiet. Until now.
The Convenient Whisper
A short time later, a small postcard arrived in the mail, the kind that looks like spam, easy to miss. It was a rent increase notice, stating that my rate was nearly tripling. A POSTCARD from a company that runs an APP, keeps my EMAIL address on file, and has no trouble reaching me ELECTRONICALLY when a payment is due.
Patterns Emerging
I had two units, each in a different state, and I noticed that the increases arrived on exactly the same schedules after move-in, even though both properties had empty units to spare. One rent hike is a landlord being a landlord. Similar hikes, on the same timeline, at unrelated facilities with vacancies all around, look like something running on a program.
The Realization
That's when I started digging. First the news stories, then the court dockets, then the academic paper written from inside the company describing its pricing model to investors, then the Better Business Bureau file, where more than a thousand strangers described the same sequence of events almost word for word. Talking with employees along the way made it plainly clear they are trained to steer every conversation away from the one honest answer, which is that the advertised rate was never going to be the price.

Why I Built This

Not to get my money back and not to settle a score. This site exists to bring the pattern out into the open, revealing that the "friendly neighborhood storage company" posing as a small business is actually Wall Street's latest heist to extract as much as possible from unsuspecting, hard-working, average everyday Americans. The people most injured by the scheme are often those who can least afford it, stripping money and/or property from the most vulnerable citizens. Forcing them through arbitration often leaves them with no recourse. On my journey of making this site, I learned what's happening is by design, and it's occurring coast to coast, affecting tens of thousands of customers who each think it is only happening to them.

The reason these stories rarely make headlines is by design too. Forced arbitration keeps disputes out of public courtrooms, and class-action bans keep customers from pooling their cases. So the evidence stays scattered and quiet.

This page puts it back in one place. If you're already stuck, maybe something here helps. If you're about to sign, take it as a warning. And if they already got you, you're not alone. Got a story of your own? It belongs here too.
