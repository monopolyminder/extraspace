Why This Site Exists

An independent consumer resource built from public records.

The Record They'd Rather You Couldn't See

This site is not affiliated with Extra Space Storage. It was built from public records: a city lawsuit, court filings, the company's own academic paper, its public statements, and more than a thousand Better Business Bureau complaints. Every claim here links to its source.

The reason these stories rarely make headlines is by design. Forced arbitration keeps disputes out of public courtrooms, and class-action bans keep customers from pooling their cases. The one service that used to help people file arbitration claims, FairShake, shut down in 2023 after companies made the process harder. So the evidence stays scattered and quiet.

So this page puts it all back in one place. If you're already stuck, maybe something here helps. If you're about to sign, take it as a warning. And if they already got you, you're not alone. Got a story of your own? It belongs here too.

The Rules

  • Every factual claim links to a source you can open and check yourself
  • Allegations are called allegations. Where a case settled, we say so, including when the company admitted nothing
  • Words like "trap" and "scam" are our opinion of what the record shows, and the disclaimer below says exactly that
  • When the record moves, the pages change, and the footer date changes with them
  • Errors get fixed, not defended. Show us one at editor@extraspacescam.com
Straight Answers

Fair Questions About This Site

A site called ExtraSpaceScam.com should expect a little skepticism. Here are the questions we'd ask, answered plainly.

Who runs this?

Consumers with no stake in the storage industry, and no byline. That's deliberate: nothing here asks you to trust us. Every claim links to a court filing, an agency release, or the company's own words, so you can check the work line by line. Credibility lives in the sources, not in a name.

Is anyone paying for this?

No. There are no ads, no affiliate links, no donation button, and nothing for sale. No law firm, competitor, or industry group had any hand in it. A static site costs almost nothing to run, which is exactly what keeps it independent.

What happens if I email my story?

A person reads it. Nothing gets published without asking you first, and anything that is gets stripped of names, locations, and account details. Even unpublished stories matter: the pattern across them is what tells us where to dig next.

Isn't "scam" too strong a word?

No court has ruled it one, and the disclaimer below says so. We use the word because a city lawsuit calls the pricing a "classic bait-and-switch scheme," a $5 million settlement followed an early auction, and 1,400+ complaints describe the same sequence. Read the sources and pick your own word.
